2025: A YEAR OF

Hayfa Sdiri · Chief Impact Officer Nadia Ali · Senior Impact Manager

Impact is often described through what is most visible: funds deployed, projects launched, people reached. These measures matter. But they rarely reflect the deeper work required to make lasting change.

For RIVET, 2025 was a year defined by that deeper work.

Throughout 2025, RIVET operated as connective tissue between youth leadership, institutions, and brands. We partnered with youth-serving organizations that bring credibility and reach within their communities. We worked with brands to unlock financial resources and non-cash support — including visibility, validation, and platforms that help young leaders be seen and taken seriously. And we listened closely to the innovators themselves, adapting our models based on what genuinely helped them move forward.

Impact is too often framed in quantitative terms that are easy to measure but difficult to feel — a proxy for the work that can obscure the human story behind grassroots change. In 2025, RIVET prioritized a more grounded understanding of impact: working to front-and-center the heart, the vision, and the drive that powers the projects we support. The people behind the project.

Our priorities have not changed. We are still building the systems and relationships that allow, grow, and hold up youth-led change. We are still working to make those systems more equitable and more accessible for those closest to the world's most urgent challenges. And we are making sure that, while we do so, it is our innovators — and their families and their communities — who guide our choices.

Youth-led innovation is already happening. Our responsibility is to ensure the systems around it are built to support it well.

03 / About RIVET

ACCELERATING YOUTH-LEDSOCIAL INNOVATIONAT SCALE

Solutions to many of the world's most urgent social, political, and environmental challenges are being formulated right now by young people who stand to be most seriously affected by them. But these solutions are embryonic and diffuse, and the route from idea to deployment has never been more obstructed.

1.8 billion young people alive today — the largest youth population in history.
85% live in capital deserts, with limited access to financial or social capital.
Less than 0.002% of global philanthropic dollars reach early-stage, youth-led initiatives.
$3 trillion in annual spending controlled by Gen Z, reshaping how value and consumption intersect with social change.

Youth innovators

RIVET is a youth innovation platform built to provide catalytic microgrants and ecosystem support to project leaders under 30 working to tackle critical global challenges. We identify leaders with real vision and help them transform early-stage ideas into scalable solutions — supported by brands, foundations, and institutions who recognise that the future depends on this generation of problem solvers.

More than funding

We don't just fund youth-led work. We redesign how youth-led work becomes fundable, visible, and sustainable.

Since 2021, RIVET has funded over 1,200 youth-led projects, supporting innovators across 79 countries. We have partnered with eight consumer brands — including Adobe, Crocs, Funko, eos and Fossil — and built an advisory network drawing from Apple, Google, Nike, adidas, and Meta.

Youth-led

And we have done all of this under the active governance of our RIVET Assembly: young decision makers with the power to approve our partnerships and shape our future direction. RIVET is not built for youth. It is built with them.

How RIVET Works

Existing funding systems were not designed with youth innovation in mind. They are too slow for moments that require rapid response, too risk-averse for early experimentation, too centralized to recognise local insight, and too focused on institutions rather than individuals closest to the issue. As a result, promising ideas fail before they can build the evidence base required to attract further support.

RIVET provides a toolkit of funding, support, and visibility that addresses these infrastructural gaps — giving early-stage innovators room to breathe, to build, and, where successful, to grow.

The CapitalPathway

RIVET structures funding across three tiers that reflect how youth-led innovation actually grows:

Tier 01

ACCESS

$250 – $999

Small, fast, and flexible. Designed for youth who have proximity to a problem and a clear idea of what to try, but lack the early capital to act. This tier reduces the gap between idea and first implementation.

Tier 02

BOOST

$1,000 – $9,999

For growing, deepening, and stabilising. Boost grants support youth innovators who have demonstrated traction and are ready to expand their reach, formalise operations, or strengthen sustainability.

Tier 03

SCALE

$10,000+

For expanding and institutionalising. Designed for youth-led initiatives ready to grow significantly — through geographic expansion, system integration, or long-term infrastructure building.

Access enables action. Boost strengthens proof. Scale accelerates durability.

Together, they create a pathway where youth can move from early experimentation to sustained growth, without being forced into premature scale or extractive models. RIVET turns youth ideas into initiatives that are fundable, trackable, and positioned to grow — while preserving youth ownership and contextual integrity.

We fund proximity. We fund urgency. We fund iteration.

CEO & Founder, RIVET

I have spent over thirty years working alongside young people who are solving problems the rest of us are still debating. What I have learned — first as a youth organizer in Ohio, then through three decades building Peace First across 160 countries — is an uncomfortable truth:

The barrier to youth-led change has never been talent. It has never been ambition. It has never been readiness.

It has been access.

Eighty-five percent of the world's young people live in capital deserts — places where brilliant ideas die quietly because there is no early funding, no institutional trust, and no pathway to legitimacy. Less than 0.002% of global philanthropic dollars reach early-stage, youth-led initiatives. That is not a talent gap. That is an infrastructure failure. And it is one of the most consequential failures in our impact ecosystem.

RIVET exists to fix that.

We did not build RIVET because the world needed another nonprofit. We built it because current funding structures are built around the needs of institutions, not individuals. Around gatekeepers, not the people closest to the problems. Around slow, risk-averse processes in a world where urgency does not wait for a grant cycle.

Young people are the only group of humans talked about almost exclusively in terms of potential. We say they are the future of leadership. The future of innovation. But that framing is wrong.

Young people are not the future of anything. They are already building the present.

A young man in rural Uganda is changing the culture of masculinity in his village with four pedal sewing machines and a $250 grant. A young woman in Gilgit-Baltistan is restoring degraded land by rethinking what a tree means for an entire community. A young engineer in Nigeria is deploying AI-powered water monitoring systems in conflict zones. These are not experiments. These are solutions. And they exist because someone believed in them early.

That early belief is what RIVET provides. We call it catalytic capital — not because the amounts are large, but because they are first. For many of the young innovators in this report, a RIVET microgrant represents the first time their work has been formally recognized by anyone outside their own community. Our data bears this out: initial microgrants have unlocked twenty to five hundred times their value in follow-on funding. When you trust youth early, capital compounds.

"If you can't be pure, be honest."
A young co-designer, RIVET early design call

These words have shaped how RIVET operates. We are not perfect. We will never fund everything that deserves to be funded. But we will always be transparent about the choices we are making and why. Young people do not need us to pretend the world is simple. They need us to be honest about its complexity and to show up anyway.

This report documents what RIVET made possible. 2025 has not been a year defined by a single breakthrough — but a year of deeper, harder work: strengthening how we grant, how we partner, how we learn from data, and how we support young innovators beyond a single moment of funding. Rather than prioritizing scale, we invested in the foundations that make scale responsible.

Every young innovator in this report carries the same conviction: that those closest to the problems are also closest to the solutions. They are not waiting for permission. They are not asking the world to be ready for them. They are already doing the work.

RIVET's job is to make sure the systems around them are built to support what they are already building. GLOSSARY OF TERMS

RIVET maintains standardised terminology across all programmes and partnerships to ensure clarity, accountability, and comparability of impact across regions and stakeholders.

Access Grant. An early-stage microgrant (<USD 1,000) designed to help a youth innovator test or launch a new idea. Provides fast, flexible catalytic capital to move from concept to initial action.

Boost Grant. A mid-stage grant (USD 1,000–9,999) designed to help a youth-led initiative expand reach, strengthen execution, or refine an existing prototype. Supports growth beyond initial validation.

Scale Grant. A higher-level grant (USD 10,000+) supporting youth-led initiatives that demonstrate traction and are ready to deepen impact, expand geographically, or institutionalise their model.

Advisory Network. Senior leaders and subject-matter experts from business, philanthropy, marketing, technology, and impact spaces providing strategic guidance, credibility, and ecosystem access.

Brand Partner. A corporate partner contributing financial and/or non-cash capital to support youth-led initiatives within the RIVET ecosystem. Brand partnerships are structured to preserve youth ownership and community-rooted impact.

Catalytic Funding. Early, flexible capital designed to unlock action, reduce friction, and signal legitimacy. Often the first institutional validation of a youth-led initiative, capable of unlocking significant follow-on investment.

Direct Beneficiaries. Individuals who directly receive services, participate in programmes, or benefit from products delivered by a RIVET-supported initiative. Reflects impact delivered by youth innovators, not RIVET staff.

Follow-On Capital. Additional funding secured by a youth innovator after receiving a RIVET grant. Reflects catalytic effect and increased institutional credibility.

Imagination Capital. The belief, legitimacy, and narrative power that allows youth innovators to be taken seriously by the broader ecosystem. Generated through visibility, storytelling, and brand amplification.

Impact Partner. A youth-serving organisation embedded within a specific community that collaborates with RIVET to identify, fund, and support young innovators locally. Impact Partners source and vet initiatives, distribute grants, provide mentorship, track impact, and surface learning.

Microgrant. A defined amount of catalytic funding deployed to support a specific youth-led initiative at the Access, Boost, or Scale level. The financial unit of measurement within RIVET's grantmaking model.

RIVET Assembly. A representative body of young leaders within RIVET that holds decision-making authority over brand and impact partnerships, ensuring youth governance and accountability remain central to ecosystem design.

Youth Innovator. An individual under 30 who leads or co-leads a RIVET-supported initiative. Because many initiatives are team-based, the number of youth innovators supported may exceed the number of grants deployed.

Youth-Led Initiative. A time-bound project, prototype, or early-stage solution led by at least one individual under 30 that addresses a clearly defined community need.

IMPACT MEASUREMENT

RIVET tracks impact using five clearly defined and non-overlapping units to ensure accountability and credibility:

Microgrants Deployed: total value of capital invested across all microgrant types

Youth-Led Initiatives Funded: number of projects or prototypes supported

Youth Innovators Supported: number of young people leading those initiatives

Direct Beneficiary Reach: number of people directly served by those initiatives

Follow-On Capital Unlocked: additional funding secured post-microgrant

Project data is captured using a three-stage process:

Baseline Survey: Administered at the start of a grant period to record demographic and geographic data, project scope, self-assessed leadership capacity, confidence, and resource gaps. Establishes the pre-support baseline against which growth and impact are measured.

Midline Survey: Used for longer grant cycles to capture progress, pivots, challenges, and execution gaps — allowing for rapid learning and adaptation.

Endline Survey: Captures strategic project metrics including budgetary performance, leadership development, emotional and social skills deltas, and supporting information for storytelling. Enables longitudinal comparison.

STRATEGIC MEASUREMENT LENSES

Output Metrics (what gets built): grants deployed, initiatives launched, services delivered, products developed, jobs created.

Outcome Metrics (who benefits): direct beneficiaries served, environmental or social improvements, communities reached.

Leadership Development Metrics (who they become): self-assessed confidence growth, leadership skills growth, collaboration capacity, resilience and agency.

Catalytic Multiplier: Follow-on capital ÷ original microgrant. Measures catalytic strength, not just funding volume. This score is assigned to every RIVET-supported project.

DATA INTEGRITY SAFEGUARDS

Distinguishing direct reach from ecosystem reach

Avoiding double-counting of beneficiaries

Requiring partner verification of critical data
